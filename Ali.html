MUHAMMAD ALI

"I AM THE GREATEST, I SAID THAT EVEN BEFORE I KNEW I WAS"

Muhammad Ali, born Cassius Clay, is regarded as one of the greatest boxers of all time. He had immense confidence in his abilities and never backed away from taking blows. His fight with George Foreman has been called "arguably the greatest sporting event of the 20th century"

His legacy though goes beyond his in ring expolits. He stood up for African-American rights. He stood up for humanitarian rights by refusing to take part in The Vietnam War. This decision cost him his peak years in his career where he was banned and stripped off his titles. But there was more to Ali than just records and titles. He used the platform he got through being boxer, to propagate the idea of being a better human.

Career Highlights
  • Winning the Gold at the Rome Olympics in 1960 at the age of 18.
  • Earned himself a fight against the mighty Sonny Liston in 1964 and emerged as the heavyweight champion for the first time.
  • Returns from his boxing exile in 1971 after being banned for 4 years to have a fight against the then heavyweight champion Joe Frazier in what is termed as The Fight of the Century. Ali lost.
  • Recalimed the title in 1974 defeating George Foreman in The Rumble in the Jungle where he famously took several blows to expend George's energy and then counter-attacked.
  • Had another famous fight Thrilla in Manilla in 1975 against Joe Frazier,their third, where Ali won and claimed that was the closest to dying he had ever been.
  • Became the first man in 1978, aged 36, to hold the heavyweight belt for the third time.

Ali was uninhibited and brutal with his thoughts. He spoke his mind. He also never shied away from proclaiming himself as the greatest. He knew he was. He will always be regarded as someone who was ruthless inside the ring, but a true icon outside it who stood up for millions of people and inspired generations to come.
